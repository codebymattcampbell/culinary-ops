# Egg First-Burn Experiment

Date: 2026-07-18

Equipment: Vision Grills Cadet / 100 Series compact kamado, kitchen name **Egg**

## Purpose

Establish baseline behavior before cooking food or adding a heat deflector.

Primary goals:

- Verify ignition and airflow
- Learn vent response
- Hold low, medium, and high dome temperatures
- Compare dome temperature with surface temperatures using an infrared thermometer
- Observe shutdown behavior
- Record any leaks, instability, or ceramic concerns

## Configuration

- No heat deflector
- Direct-fire setup
- Stainless cooking grate installed
- Lump charcoal
- Small chimney starter used
- Initial fuel load reduced after the chimney was judged too full for a low-temperature control test

## Ignition Notes

1. The first paper load mostly smoldered and produced heavy white smoke instead of a sustained flame.
2. The paper had been packed too tightly and restricted its own airflow.
3. The paper was loosened/reduced and relit with better access to the chimney's lower air openings.
4. A small group of charcoal pieces eventually showed ember glow and light gray ash.
5. The lit charcoal was dumped into the center of the unlit lump bed.
6. The original lit pocket did not make sufficient contact with the surrounding lump, so the charcoal bed had to be adjusted manually.
7. After the adjustment, the dome temperature began climbing reliably toward 200°F.

## Early Observations

- A small starter load is sufficient once the lit pieces physically contact the unlit fuel.
- The kamado initially climbed slowly because the lit pocket was isolated.
- Paper smoke was dense and dirty at first, then diminished once charcoal ignition became established.
- Most of the lump remained black and unlit during the early climb, which is desirable for a controlled burn.
- Bottom and top vents were initially left fully open.

## Planned Temperature Sequence

1. Hold 250-275°F for 20-30 minutes.
2. Raise to approximately 350°F and hold for 20 minutes.
3. Raise to approximately 500-550°F for 10-15 minutes.
4. Close both vents completely and observe cooldown and fire extinction.

The test will not intentionally chase the absolute maximum temperature on the first burn.

## Infrared Thermometer Plan

At dome targets near 250°F, 350°F, and 500°F, measure the same locations from approximately the same distance:

- Center of cooking grate
- Outer edge of cooking grate
- Inside ceramic wall below grate level
- Outside dome near the built-in thermometer
- Outside lower body near the firebox

Caution: shiny stainless grate rods may produce unreliable infrared readings. Prefer darkened or sooty targets when available.

## Safety Notes

- Above approximately 400°F, burp the lid before opening fully.
- Do not pour water into the cooker.
- Allow ash and charcoal to cool completely before disposal.

## First IR Profile Interpretation

- The center grate measured approximately 125°F hotter than the dome gauge, consistent with strong direct radiant heating over the active charcoal.
- The outer grate measured much cooler than the center, confirming a pronounced direct-fire hot spot and a naturally cooler perimeter.
- The inside wall, outside dome, and lower-body readings remained comparatively low because the ceramic had not yet fully heat-soaked.
- This profile supports using a heat deflector for roasting and low-and-slow cooking and suggests the future deflector should cover most of the central radiant path while leaving a perimeter gap for airflow.
