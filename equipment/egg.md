# Egg

Our small orange ceramic kamado grill. "Egg" is the kitchen name we use for it.

## What It Is

A charcoal-fired ceramic cooker that retains heat and moisture well and can operate as a grill, smoker, or outdoor oven.

## Best Uses

- Direct grilling
- Low-and-slow barbecue
- Roasting with indirect heat
- High-temperature searing

## Capabilities

- Broad temperature range controlled primarily through airflow
- Strong heat retention once the ceramic is stabilized
- Direct and indirect cooking depending on accessories

## Limitations

- Temperature changes happen slowly after the ceramic heats up
- Small vent adjustments can have a large effect
- Limited cooking area compared with a full-size kamado
- Indirect cooking requires a correctly sized heat deflector

## Safety

- Open the lid slowly after high-temperature cooking to reduce flashback risk.
- Keep the cooker on a stable, noncombustible surface.
- Do not move it while hot.
- Confirm vents are clear before lighting.
- Allow charcoal and ash to cool completely before disposal.

## Care and Maintenance

- Remove excess ash so airflow remains predictable.
- Inspect the firebox and ceramic body for cracks.
- Keep metal hardware dry and address corrosion early.
- Avoid rapid temperature shocks to hot ceramic.
