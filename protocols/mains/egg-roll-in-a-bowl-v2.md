# MAIN - Egg Roll in a Bowl v2

## Metadata

- Type: MAIN
- Cuisine: Chinese-American inspired
- Status: Draft
- Health Rating: Everyday
- Servings: 4-6
- Serving:
  - Calories: ~430 without rice
  - Protein: ~33 g
  - Carbs: ~15 g without rice
  - Fat: ~28 g
  - Fiber: ~5-6 g

## Equipment

- Blackstone or large skillet
- Cutting board
- Chef's knife

## Meal Role

- Standalone main
- Optional base: brown rice

## Ingredients

- 2 lb lean ground pork, turkey, or chicken
- 1/2 to 2/3 head green cabbage, thinly sliced
- 1 yellow onion, sliced or diced
- Garlic, minced
- Fresh ginger, minced or grated
- 2 cans sliced water chestnuts, drained
- Green onions, sliced
- Roasted unsalted cashews, for garnish
- Sesame seeds, optional

## Sauce Direction

- Soy sauce
- Fish sauce
- Rice vinegar
- Toasted sesame oil
- Black pepper or white pepper
- Optional small drizzle honey or pinch brown sugar
- Optional chili crisp or hot sauce at the table

## Method Sketch

1. Brown the ground meat and develop some color.
2. Add onion, garlic, and ginger.
3. Add cabbage and cook until wilted but not dead-soft.
4. Add water chestnuts near the end to preserve crunch.
5. Finish with sauce, green onions, cashews, and sesame seeds.
6. Serve over brown rice for those who want it.

## Design Notes

- Water chestnuts solve the crunch problem without adding many calories.
- Cashews add richness and texture but should stay as a garnish to control calories.
- Fish sauce increases umami without turning the dish heavy.
