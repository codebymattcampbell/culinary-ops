# MAIN - East African Spiced Braised Lamb

## Metadata

- Type: MAIN
- Cuisine: East African inspired
- Status: Draft
- Health Rating: Balanced
- Servings: 6
- Serving:
  - Calories: ~575
  - Protein: ~40 g
  - Carbs: ~20 g without bread
  - Fat: ~34 g
  - Fiber: ~5 g

## Equipment

- Dutch oven or Instant Pot
- Stove

## Meal Role

Pair with:

- Ethiopian cabbage / atakilt wat
- Tomato cucumber salad
- Lemon herb yogurt
- Skillet flatbread
- Berbere and harissa at the table

## Ingredients

- 2.5-3 lb lamb shoulder or lamb stew meat
- Substitution: beef chuck if lamb does not look good or is overpriced
- Yellow onion
- Garlic
- Fresh ginger
- Tomato paste
- Broth or water
- Cumin
- Coriander
- Turmeric
- Cardamom
- Cinnamon
- Salt
- Black pepper

## Heat Strategy

- Keep the braise mild to medium.
- Serve berbere and harissa at the table so each person can adjust heat.

## Method Sketch

1. Brown lamb in batches.
2. Sweat onion, garlic, and ginger.
3. Bloom spices and tomato paste.
4. Add lamb back with enough liquid to braise.
5. Cook until fork tender.
6. Adjust seasoning and serve with bright/cooling sides.

## Design Notes

- The lamb is the rich and savory anchor.
- Acid and cooling elements should come from salad and yogurt, not from making the braise sharp.
