# Experiment - Japanese Milk Bread - v1

## Metadata

- Related Recipe: [Japanese Milk Bread](../../../recipes/breads/japanese-milk-bread.md)
- Status: Completed
- Version: v1

## Hypothesis

A tangzhong-based Japanese milk bread can be produced successfully on Course 1 of the Zojirushi BB-PDC20 using diluted half-and-half instead of whole milk.

## Variables

- Changed: Whole milk replaced with half-and-half and water.
- Held constant: Flour, egg, butter, sugar, salt, yeast, tangzhong temperature target, bread-machine course, and crust setting.

## Equipment

- Zojirushi BB-PDC20 bread machine
- Small saucepan
- Whisk
- Digital scale

## Inputs

| Input | Value |
|---|---|
| Bread flour | 380 g total, including tangzhong |
| Half-and-half | 60 g total |
| Water | 180 g total |
| Egg | Approximately 50 g |
| Butter | 35 g |
| Sugar | 30 g |
| Salt | 8 g |
| Instant yeast | 5 g |
| Tangzhong endpoint | Approximately 149°F / 65°C, with glossy visible trails |
| Bread-machine course | 1 — Basic White |
| Crust | Light |

## Observations

- Tangzhong became smooth, glossy, and thick enough to hold whisk trails.
- Dough formed a cohesive, smooth ball and cleared most of the pan without flour or water correction.
- The machine rested before kneading; this was normal behavior.
- Finished loaf had excellent volume and did not collapse.
- Side and bottom crust browned evenly on the Light setting.
- Top was folded and irregular, likely from bread-machine shaping, but the structure remained sound.
- Crumb was soft, even, moist, and free of a gummy or compressed lower band.
- Flavor was delicious despite the milk substitution.
