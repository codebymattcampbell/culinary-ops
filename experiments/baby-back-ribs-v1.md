# Experiment - Baby Back Ribs v1

## Metadata

- Date: 2026-06
- Related Protocol: Ribs on Big Green Egg
- Status: Completed / Revisit
- Cook Version: v1

## Hypothesis

A low-and-slow Big Green Egg rib cook would produce tender ribs with good texture across the rack.

## Variables

- Equipment: Big Green Egg
- Target pit temperature: ~250 F
- Actual temperature: drifted higher at points, up to roughly 350 F
- Meat: pork loin back / baby back ribs

## Observations

- Final ribs were slightly overdone.
- Meat stuck to the bone more than desired.
- Center sections had the best texture.
- Thin end sections overcooked.
- Some thinner sections became crunchy when reheated days later.

## Results

- What worked: center rack texture and flavor were good.
- What did not work: thin-end management and overall doneness control.
- Surprise: leftovers exaggerated the overcooked texture in thin sections.

## Next Iteration

- Protect or remove thin ends earlier.
- Check doneness across multiple parts of the rack, not just the center.
- Pull based on texture variance across the rack.
- Revisit lower and steadier pit temperature management.
