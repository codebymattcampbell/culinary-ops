# East African-Spiced Braised Lamb

A mild-to-medium spiced lamb braise designed to be served with bright, cooling sides and adjustable heat at the table.

## Status

**Experimental**

## Yield

Approximately 6 servings.

## Required Equipment

- [Instant Pot](../../equipment/instant-pot.md) or Dutch oven
- [Stovetop](../../equipment/stovetop.md)

## Ingredients

- 2.5–3 lb lamb shoulder or lamb stew meat
- Beef chuck, optional substitute
- Yellow onion
- Garlic
- Fresh ginger
- Tomato paste
- Broth or water
- Cumin
- Coriander
- Turmeric
- Cardamom
- Cinnamon
- Salt
- Black pepper

## Method

1. Brown the lamb in batches.
2. Soften the onion, garlic, and ginger.
3. Bloom the spices and tomato paste.
4. Return the lamb and add enough liquid to braise.
5. Cook until fork-tender.
6. Adjust the seasoning and serve with bright or cooling sides.

## Heat Strategy

- Keep the braise itself mild to medium.
- Offer berbere or harissa at the table so each person can control the heat.

## Suggested Pairings

- Ethiopian cabbage or atakilt wat
- Tomato cucumber salad
- Lemon herb yogurt
- Skillet flatbread

## Notes

- The lamb should be the rich, savory anchor.
- Acidity and cooling contrast should come primarily from the sides rather than making the braise sharply acidic.
- Exact spice and liquid quantities and cooking time still need validation.
