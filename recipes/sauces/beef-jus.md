# Beef Jus

## At a Glance

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Prep Time | 5 minutes |
| Cook Time | 15–20 minutes |
| Difficulty | Easy |
| Yield | Approximately 1 cup |
| Primary Technique | Reduction |
| Supporting Techniques | Deglazing, Emulsification |

---

## Description

A rich, concentrated beef jus built from sous vide cooking juices and finished with cold butter for a glossy, silky texture. Designed to complement roasted meats without becoming overly thick or heavy.

---

## Equipment

- Small saucepan
- Fine mesh strainer (optional)
- Silicone spatula or wooden spoon
- Whisk

---

## Ingredients

### Base

- Reserved juices from 1 sous vide chuck roast
- 1 shallot, finely minced

### Liquid

- ½ cup low-sodium beef stock (as needed)
- ¼ cup dry red wine (optional)

### Finishing

- 1 tbsp cold unsalted butter

### Seasoning

- Fresh thyme (1–2 sprigs)
- Freshly ground black pepper

---

## Procedure

### 1. Build the Base (3–5 min)

Place the saucepan over medium heat.

Add:

- Reserved sous vide juices
- Minced shallot
- Red wine (if using)
- Thyme

Bring to a gentle simmer.

---

### 2. Reduce (10–15 min)

Reduce the liquid until it has intensified in flavor and reduced by approximately one-third to one-half.

If additional volume is needed, add beef stock gradually during the reduction.

Remove the thyme before finishing.

---

### 3. Finish (1 min)

Reduce the heat to low.

Whisk in the cold butter until fully incorporated.

Season with freshly ground black pepper.

Taste before adding salt, as the bag juices are often already well seasoned.

Serve immediately.

---

## Yield

Makes approximately **1 cup**.

Recommended serving:

- 2–3 tbsp per person

---

---

## Storage

Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to **4 days**.

Reheat gently over low heat.

Do not boil after the butter has been incorporated, as the emulsion may break.

---

## Notes

- The bag juices are the primary source of flavor.
- Use low-sodium beef stock so you remain in control of seasoning.
- Cold butter produces a smoother emulsion than warm butter.
- This is intended to remain a light jus, not a thick gravy.

---

## Quality Checks

Before Serving

- Sauce lightly coats the back of a spoon.
- Glossy appearance.
- Concentrated beef flavor without excessive saltiness.
- Shallots are completely softened.
- Butter is fully incorporated with no visible separation.
- Consistency remains pourable.
