# Horseradish Cream

## At a Glance

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Prep Time | 10 minutes |
| Cook Time | None |
| Difficulty | Easy |
| Yield | Approximately 1 cup |
| Primary Technique | Emulsification |
| Supporting Techniques | Seasoning, Resting |

---

## Description

A bright, creamy horseradish sauce that complements rich beef without overpowering it. Resting the sauce before serving allows the flavors to meld while preserving the horseradish's sharp bite.

---

## Equipment

- Medium mixing bowl
- Whisk
- Microplane or fine grater
- Rubber spatula

---

## Ingredients

### Base

- 1 cup sour cream

### Flavoring

- 3 tbsp prepared horseradish, drained
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tsp fresh lemon juice
- ½ tsp lemon zest

### Seasoning

- Freshly ground black pepper
- Kosher salt, to taste

### Optional

- 1 tbsp chopped fresh chives

---

## Procedure

### 1. Combine (5 min)

Add the sour cream, horseradish, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, and lemon zest to a mixing bowl.

Whisk until smooth and evenly combined.

---

### 2. Season (2 min)

Season with freshly ground black pepper.

Taste before adding salt, then season lightly if needed.

Fold in chopped chives, if using.

---

### 3. Rest (30 min minimum)

Cover and refrigerate for at least **30 minutes**.

Stir once before serving.

---

## Yield

Makes approximately **1 cup**.

Recommended serving:

- 1–2 tbsp per person

## Storage

Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to **5 days**.

Do not freeze.

Stir before serving if any separation occurs.

---

## Culinary Ops Notes

- Prepared horseradish gradually loses its pungency after opening. Fresh jars produce noticeably brighter sauces.
- Resting allows the lemon, mustard, and horseradish to integrate without dulling the overall flavor.
- Sour cream provides tang while keeping the sauce lighter than mayonnaise-based versions.
- Add salt sparingly—the horseradish and mustard often contribute enough seasoning.

---

## Quality Checks

Before Serving

- Smooth, creamy texture
- Horseradish is immediately noticeable but not overwhelming
- Bright acidity from the lemon
- Thick enough to stay on a slice of beef
- No visible liquid separation
