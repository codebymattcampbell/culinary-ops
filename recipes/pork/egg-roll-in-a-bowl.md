# Egg Roll in a Bowl

A cabbage-forward skillet meal with ground meat, aromatics, water chestnuts, and a savory soy-fish-sauce finish.

## Status

**Experimental**

## Yield

4–6 servings.

## Required Equipment

- Large skillet or Blackstone
- Cutting board
- Chef's knife

## Ingredients

- 2 lb lean ground pork, turkey, or chicken
- 1/2–2/3 head green cabbage, thinly sliced
- 1 yellow onion, sliced or diced
- Garlic, minced
- Fresh ginger, minced or grated
- 2 cans sliced water chestnuts, drained
- Green onions, sliced
- Roasted unsalted cashews, for garnish
- Sesame seeds, optional

### Sauce Direction

- Soy sauce
- Fish sauce
- Rice vinegar
- Toasted sesame oil
- Black or white pepper
- Honey or brown sugar, optional
- Chili crisp or hot sauce, optional for serving

## Method

1. Brown the ground meat and develop some color.
2. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger.
3. Add the cabbage and cook until wilted but not soft and lifeless.
4. Add the water chestnuts near the end to preserve their crunch.
5. Finish with the sauce, green onions, cashews, and optional sesame seeds.
6. Serve alone or over brown rice.

## Quality Targets

- Cabbage tender but still structured
- Water chestnuts crisp
- Savory and aromatic without becoming greasy or overly salty

## Notes

- Cashews should remain a garnish so their richness and calories stay controllable.
- Exact sauce quantities still need validation.
