# Georgian Herb & Walnut Salad

## At a Glance

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Prep Time | 20 minutes |
| Cook Time | 5 minutes |
| Difficulty | Easy |
| Yield | 4 servings |
| Primary Technique | Fresh Preparation |
| Supporting Techniques | Toasting, Emulsification |

---

## Description

A bright, herb-forward salad inspired by Georgian flavors. Fresh vegetables, toasted walnuts, and a light lemon vinaigrette provide acidity, freshness, and texture to balance rich roasted meats.

---

## Equipment

- Chef's knife
- Cutting board
- Dry skillet
- Large mixing bowl
- Small bowl or mason jar for dressing
- Whisk

---

## Ingredients

### Salad

- 2 ripe tomatoes, cut into wedges
- 1 English cucumber, halved lengthwise and sliced
- ¼ red onion, thinly sliced
- ½ cup flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
- ¼ cup fresh dill, chopped
- 2 tbsp fresh mint, chopped (optional)
- ½ cup walnuts

### Dressing

- 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 garlic clove, finely grated
- 1 tsp honey (optional)

### Seasoning

- Kosher salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Zest of ½ lemon

---

## Procedure

### 1. Toast the Walnuts (5 min)

Toast the walnuts in a dry skillet over medium heat until fragrant.

Allow to cool before roughly chopping.

Reserve a small handful for garnish.

---

### 2. Prepare the Vegetables (10 min)

Slice the cucumber.

Cut the tomatoes into wedges.

Thinly slice the red onion.

Roughly chop the parsley, dill, and mint.

Transfer everything to a large mixing bowl.

---

### 3. Prepare the Dressing (2 min)

Whisk together:

- Olive oil
- Lemon juice
- Red wine vinegar
- Dijon mustard
- Garlic
- Honey (if using)

Season with black pepper.

Taste before adding salt.

---

### 4. Dress the Salad (2 min)

Add the walnuts.

Pour the dressing over the salad.

Toss gently until evenly coated.

Finish with:

- Lemon zest
- Reserved toasted walnuts
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Flaky salt (optional)

Serve immediately.

---

## Yield

Makes approximately **4 servings**.

Recommended serving:

- 1 generous cup (approximately 180–200 g)

## Storage

Best served immediately after dressing.

Prepared vegetables and dressing may be stored separately for up to **2 days**.

Do not dress the salad until ready to serve.

---

## Culinary Ops Notes

- Toasting walnuts significantly improves both flavor and texture.
- Dress the salad immediately before serving to preserve freshness.
- Lemon and vinegar provide acidity that balances rich meat dishes.
- Fresh herbs should be generous; they are a defining characteristic of the salad rather than a garnish.

---

## Quality Checks

Before Serving

- Vegetables remain crisp.
- Herbs are vibrant and unwilted.
- Walnuts are fragrant and crunchy.
- Dressing lightly coats the vegetables without pooling.
- Bright acidity balances the richness of accompanying dishes.
- Fresh herbs remain the dominant aroma.
