# Yorkshire Pudding

## At a Glance

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Prep Time | 10 minutes |

---

## Description

Classic Yorkshire puddings with crisp, deeply browned exteriors and light, airy interiors. Designed for individual servings in a standard muffin tin.

---

## Equipment

- Standard 12-cup metal muffin tin
- Mixing bowl
- Whisk
- Measuring cups
- Ladle or measuring cup for pouring batter

---

## Ingredients

### Batter

- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 cup whole milk
- 3 large eggs
- ½ tsp kosher salt

### Pan

- 8 tsp beef fat (preferred) or high smoke point oil

---

## Procedure

### 1. Make the Batter (10 min)

Whisk together:

- Flour
- Milk
- Eggs
- Salt

Whisk until completely smooth.

Cover and refrigerate for **30 minutes to 2 hours**.

---

### 2. Preheat (20 min)

Preheat oven to **450°F (230°C)**.

Place the empty muffin tin in the oven.

When fully heated, add **1 teaspoon** of beef fat to each of **8** muffin cups.

Return the pan to the oven until the fat is shimmering and nearly smoking.

---

### 3. Fill

Remove the pan from the oven.

Stir the batter.

Fill each prepared cup approximately **⅓ to ½ full**.

Return the pan to the oven immediately.

---

### 4. Bake (20–25 min)

Bake until deeply browned and well puffed.

Avoid opening the oven during the first **18–20 minutes**.

---

### 5. Serve

Serve immediately while hot and crisp.

---

## Yield

Makes **8 individual Yorkshire puddings**.

Recommended serving:

- 1–2 puddings per person.

## Storage

Best served immediately.

Refrigerate leftovers for up to **3 days**.

Reheat in a **400°F oven** for 5–7 minutes.

---

## Culinary Ops Notes

- The dramatic rise comes primarily from steam generated by the liquid in the batter.
- A hot pan and hot fat begin cooking the batter immediately, encouraging maximum lift.
- Beef fat produces the most traditional flavor, but neutral oils work well.
- Resting the batter allows the flour to fully hydrate, improving consistency.

---

## Quality Checks

Before Serving

- Well puffed with distinct hollow centers.
- Deep golden brown exterior.
- Crisp outside.
- Tender, slightly custardy interior.
- No raw batter remaining in the center.
