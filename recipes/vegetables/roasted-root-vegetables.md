# Roasted Root Vegetables

## At a Glance

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Prep Time | 20 minutes |
| Cook Time | 35–45 minutes |
| Difficulty | Easy |
| Servings | 4 |
| Primary Technique | Roasting |
| Supporting Techniques | Caramelization |

---

## Description

A versatile roasted vegetable medley that develops deep caramelization while preserving the natural sweetness and texture of each vegetable. Designed to accompany roasted meats without overpowering them.

---

## Equipment

- Half-sheet pan
- Large mixing bowl
- Chef's knife
- Cutting board
- Vegetable peeler
- Metal spatula or fish turner

---

## Ingredients

### Vegetables

- 2 lb carrots
- 1½ lb parsnips
- 3–4 shallots
- 1 head garlic

### Seasoning

- 2 tbsp avocado oil
- Kosher salt
- Freshly ground black pepper

### Finishing

- 1 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp chopped fresh thyme
- 1 tsp chopped fresh rosemary

---

## Procedure

### 1. Preheat

Preheat oven to **425°F (220°C)**.

Place a half-sheet pan in the oven while it preheats.

---

### 2. Prepare the Vegetables

Peel the carrots and parsnips.

Cut into similar-sized pieces, approximately **1½–2 inches**.

Halve the shallots through the root.

Separate the garlic cloves, leaving the skins on.

---

### 3. Season

Transfer the vegetables to a large mixing bowl.

Add:

- Avocado oil
- Kosher salt
- Freshly ground black pepper

Toss until evenly coated.

---

### 4. Roast

Carefully remove the hot sheet pan from the oven.

Spread the vegetables into a single layer.

Roast for **35–45 minutes**, turning once halfway through cooking.

The vegetables are done when:

- Deeply caramelized around the edges
- Easily pierced with a knife
- Tender but not mushy

---

### 5. Finish

Transfer the vegetables to a serving bowl.

Add:

- Butter
- Thyme
- Rosemary

Gently toss until the butter has melted and evenly coats the vegetables.

Serve immediately.

---

## Yield

Makes approximately **4 servings**.

Recommended serving:

- 8 oz (225 g) roasted vegetables

## Storage

Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to **4 days**.

Reheat in a **400°F oven** for 10–15 minutes for the best texture.

Avoid microwaving when possible, as it softens the caramelized exterior.

---

## Notes

- Similar-sized pieces promote even cooking.
- A preheated sheet pan encourages immediate browning instead of steaming.
- Avoid overcrowding the pan. Use two sheet pans if necessary.
- Leaving the garlic in its skin protects it from burning while allowing it to become soft and sweet.
- Add the butter and fresh herbs after roasting to preserve their flavor.
