# Sous Vide Chuck Roast

## At a Glance

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Prep Time | 20 minutes |
| Cook Time | 20 hours |
| Servings | 3 |
| Difficulty | Easy |
| Primary Technique | Sous Vide |
| Supporting Techniques | Final Sear, Resting |

## Description

Transform an inexpensive chuck roast into a tender, steak-like centerpiece using low-temperature sous vide cooking followed by a high-heat final sear. This method maximizes moisture retention while allowing time for collagen to convert into gelatin, producing a roast with exceptional tenderness and a uniform medium-rare interior.

---

## Equipment

- Sous vide circulator
- Vacuum sealer and bags
- Large cooking vessel
- Cast iron skillet or heavy stainless steel pan
- Tongs
- Paper towels

---

## Ingredients

### Beef

- 1 boneless chuck roast (approximately 2 lb, about 2 inches thick)

### Seasoning

- Kosher salt
- Freshly ground black pepper

### Optional Aromatics

- Fresh thyme
- Garlic cloves

### For Searing

- High smoke point oil (avocado oil recommended)

---

## Procedure

### 1. Season

Season the roast generously on all sides with kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper.

Add optional aromatics if desired and vacuum seal.

---

### 2. Sous Vide

Preheat the water bath to **137°F (58.3°C)**.

Cook for **20 hours**.

---

### 3. Dry

Remove the roast from the bag.

Reserve all cooking juices for beef jus.

Pat the roast completely dry with paper towels.

For an even better crust, refrigerate uncovered for 20–30 minutes before searing.

---

### 4. Final Sear

Heat a cast iron skillet until very hot.

Add a thin film of high smoke point oil.

Sear:

- 45–60 seconds per side
- Briefly sear all edges

The goal is to brown the exterior without raising the internal temperature.

---

### 5. Rest

Rest for approximately 5 minutes.

Slice across the grain into ¼–½ inch slices.

Serve immediately.

## Recommended Pairings

### Sauces

- Beef Jus

### Sides

- Roasted Root Vegetables

---

## Storage

Refrigerate for up to 4 days.

For best results, reheat gently in a sous vide bath or low oven.

---

## Notes

- Chuck roast is rich in collagen, making it exceptionally well suited for long sous vide cooks.
- Drying the exterior thoroughly before searing is critical for rapid browning.
- Reserve all bag juices for use in a pan sauce or jus.
- Slice across the grain to maximize tenderness.
