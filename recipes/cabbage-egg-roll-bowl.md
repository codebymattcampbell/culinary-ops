# Cabbage Egg Roll Bowl

A quick cabbage-heavy egg roll filling without the wrapper. Good for a fast dinner, meal prep, or using up cabbage.

## Yield

4 servings

## Ingredients

- 1 lb ground pork, ground chicken, ground turkey, or shrimp
- 1 small green cabbage, thinly sliced, or 1 large bag coleslaw mix
- 1 medium carrot, grated or julienned
- 1 small onion, thinly sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated or minced
- 2 tbsp neutral oil, divided
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
- 1 tsp sriracha or chili crisp, optional
- 2 green onions, sliced
- Toasted sesame seeds, optional
- Kosher salt and black pepper, to taste

## Optional sauce

- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
- 1 tsp honey or brown sugar, optional
- Chili crisp, sriracha, or gochujang, to taste

## Method

1. Prep everything first. This cooks quickly once the pan is hot.
2. Heat 1 tbsp neutral oil in a large skillet, wok, or Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
3. Add the meat and cook until browned and cooked through. Season lightly with salt and pepper. Remove excess rendered fat if needed, but leave a little for flavor.
4. Push the meat to one side or remove it to a bowl. Add the remaining 1 tbsp oil, then add onion, garlic, and ginger. Cook for 30 to 60 seconds until fragrant.
5. Add cabbage and carrot. Toss well so the cabbage gets coated in the fat and aromatics.
6. Cook 4 to 7 minutes, stirring often, until the cabbage softens but still has a little bite. Avoid steaming it into mush.
7. Stir in soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and sriracha or chili crisp if using.
8. Taste and adjust with more soy sauce, vinegar, salt, pepper, or heat.
9. Finish with green onions and sesame seeds.

## Notes

- For a drier, more browned version, cook in a wider pan and avoid overcrowding.
- For a softer, more takeout-style filling, cover the pan for 2 to 3 minutes after adding the cabbage, then uncover and reduce any extra liquid.
- Serve as-is, over rice, with cauliflower rice, or wrapped in lettuce.
- If using shrimp, cook it separately until just done, remove it, then add it back at the end so it does not overcook.
